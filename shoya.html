Shoya Ishida

The troubled, guilt-ridden protagonist of A Silent Voice, whose journey centers on redemption and personal growth

Shōya Ishida is a deeply layered and emotionally compelling character from A Silent Voice, whose story revolves around guilt, growth, and the search for self-forgiveness. As a child, he bullied Shōko Nishimiya without understanding the pain he caused, but when the consequences finally fell on him, he became isolated, ashamed, and burdened by regret. His later life is shaped by this heavy guilt, driving him to change, rebuild his character, and make amends. Shōya’s journey is powerful because it shows how someone can confront their worst mistakes, learn empathy, and slowly heal. His transformation from a reckless boy into a thoughtful, fragile yet determined young man makes him one of the most realistic and relatable characters in modern anime.
