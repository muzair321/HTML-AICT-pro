Shoko Nishimiya

The gentle, deaf heroine of A Silent Voice, known for her kindness and emotional resilience

Shōko Nishimiya is a quiet yet incredibly strong character whose presence shapes the emotional heart of A Silent Voice. Born deaf, she faces constant misunderstanding and bullying, yet she remains compassionate and willing to forgive—even those who once hurt her. Her struggles with self-worth and communication reveal a deep vulnerability, but also a powerful determination to connect with others. Shōko’s journey is about learning to value herself, finding her voice in her own way, and breaking free from the guilt she carries. Her gentle personality, resilience, and warmth have made her one of the most beloved and memorable characters in modern anime.
